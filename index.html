About

Westscribe is a professional practice focused on Turkish–English / English–Turkish translation, dubbing script adaptation, and subtitle production for audiovisual content across a wide range of genres and formats.

Led by Batıkan Bahadır Ünsal, with over 10 years of industry experience, Westscribe has completed over 500 projects and delivered nearly 5,000 individual content assets for platforms including Netflix, Amazon Prime, Disney+, HBO Max, Discovery, BBC, and leading local broadcasters.

In Türkiye, Westscribe has collaborated on projects with various dubbing and localization studios, including Iyuno (formerly Ak'la Kara), VSI, Lokalist, Studio DSE, Recart, Plint, and SDS.

Focus

Westscribe works exclusively with text.

Text created to be spoken, recorded, and realized on screen.

The focus is clear and deliberately defined.
Westscribe does not provide end-to-end operations, nor does it claim ownership of the entire production chain.

The scope of work includes:

Dubbing Scripts

Sync-aware, performable texts written with timing, mouth movements, and actor performance in mind.

Subtitles

Readable, accurate subtitle texts prepared in compliance with platform-specific technical standards and timing requirements.

Transcription & Adaptation

Accurate source texts and adapted scripts prepared for further localization, dubbing, or subtitle production.

This approach treats text not as an intermediate step, but as a core production element; one that must work seamlessly on stage, in the booth, and on screen.

Approach

For Westscribe, good translation is;

not defined by the source text alone, but by how the text performs in practice.

It is writing that actors can inhabit comfortably,
that supports the director and recording crew,
and that preserves the tone, rhythm, and intent of the original work.

Approach varies by context.

In documentary and academic content, technical accuracy and fidelity to the source are essential.

In series, films, comedy, and animation, localization, dialog flow, and creative adaptation take priority.

Characters

Characters are treated as individuals, each with a distinct voice. The text is shaped first for performance, so that recording can proceed smoothly and naturally.

Lip Sync

Lip sync is not an afterthought. A translation is considered complete only when it meets the voice naturally; effortless to perform, seamless in recording, and convincing on screen.

Workflow & Trust

Speed & Delivery

Westscribe is structured to work reliably under tight delivery schedules. Same-day delivery, night and weekend workflows are part of standard practice when required.

Speed is never treated as a shortcut. It is the result of a disciplined production process, a mature workflow refined over years, and decisions made early to protect quality throughout the project.

Depending on complexity, the delivery of more than two 45-minute episodes per day is achievable without compromising accuracy or performance.

Revision & Quality Control

Revision workflows are fast and controlled. QC feedback is handled carefully; approved texts are re-evaluated when needed, incorrect changes are rolled back, and alternative solutions are proposed with clarity.

Quality, delivery discipline, and the text's ability to meet the voice naturally remain constant.

Security & NDA

All materials are handled in full compliance with NDA requirements. Video and audio files are deleted upon project completion. Delivered texts are retained securely, with controlled access and no external sharing.
